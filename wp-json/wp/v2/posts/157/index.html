If you think your data is safe from hackers because you have a strong firewall and antivirus software, think again. You may be overlooking one of the biggest threats to your data security: your own employees. According to a study by IBM, 60% of cyberattacks in 2015 were carried out by insiders, either intentionally or unintentionally. This means that the users who have access to your data may be the ones who are destroying it, either for personal gain, revenge, or sabotage. Fraud and theft are two types of internal attacks that can cause serious damage to data integrity and security.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Internal threats vs External threats<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

According to a report by Verizon<\/a>, 30% of data breaches in 2020 involved internal actors. These actors may have various motives for stealing or destroying data, such as financial gain, revenge, sabotage, or espionage. Unlike external attacks, internal attacks are often harder to detect and prevent, as the perpetrators may have legitimate credentials and permissions to access the data. An example of internal attack in cyber security is in 2016, a disgruntled employee of a web hosting company deleted 1,500 customer websites and backups, causing irreversible damage and forcing the company to shut down.<\/p>\n\n\n
