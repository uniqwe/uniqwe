If you are a system administrator or a developer, you know how important it is to maintain the integrity of your system. Integrity means that the data and the functionality of the system are accurate, consistent, and reliable. However, integrity can be easily compromised by technology errors and omissions, which are the number-one threat to the system.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Save Your System from the Biggest Threat: Yourself<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
\n

Errors and omissions<\/a> are mistakes or oversights that occur when users interact with the system. They can be intentional or unintentional, malicious or benign, but they all have the potential to damage or destroy data, corrupt files, introduce bugs, or cause downtime.<\/p>\n<\/div><\/div>\n\n\n\n

For example, a user might accidentally delete a crucial file, overwrite a configuration setting, enter invalid data, or forget to perform a necessary task. However, if the users intentionally modify the data or destroy the information, it is called an internal threat<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n
